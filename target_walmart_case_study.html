Target vs. Walmart

A comparative analysis on the information architecture and navigation of two e-commerce websites.

Objective

The objective of this study is to do a comparative analysis on the information architecture and navigation of two e-commerce websites, Walmart and Target. Both website’s information architecture will be evaluated through the following metrics:

  • Task Success: Was the user able to find the products through the website’s navigation and Information Architecture?
  • Efficiency: How many steps did the participant take to find the product?
  • Self Reported Metrics: How was the overall satisfaction and the user’s overall experience percieved?
  • Comparative Metrics: How do both website’s above metrics compare?

Overview

It is slightly easier to find products in the Target navigation than the Walmart navigation. Out of all the tasks completed by participants, 11% more participants ended up on the ‘correct’ product from the Target navigation than the Walmart navigation. Task directness, or answers chosen by participants without backtracking, is higher in the Target study by 5%.

Methodology

Two tree jack studies (one for each site) were conducted through Optimal Workshop by the same participants. Participants were asked to find the same 6 products on both studies. Afterwards, participants were asked to evaluate how easy it was to find the products on each site and to identify which products they thought were hard to find.

Findings and Recommendations

01. Task and Success Criteria

You would like to purchase an apple watch series 5 GPS. Where would you look to find this product?

  • Target Success Criteria: Categories / Electronics / Wearable Technology / Smartwatches
  • Walmart Success Criteria: Electronics & Office / Wearable Tech / Apple Watch

Results

  • Target
    • Direct Success: 62%
    • Indirect Success: 23%
    • Direct Failure: 15%
  • Walmart
    • Direct Success: 100%

Finding 1

Users shop for apple watches in the sports and deals section.

In the Target study, the failed paths were:

  • Categories / Sports and Outdoors / Exercise & Fitness 
  • Deals / Top Deals 

This is where participants clicked first:

  • Categories (69%) Correct first click
  • Deals (15%)
  • What’s new (8%)
  • More options (8%)

Recommendation 1

Include wearable technology in the sports and fitness categories.

Since people use apple watches for fitness purposes, it would be good to include this product in the Exercise & Fitness category on the Target navigation.

02. Task and Success Criteria

You  are in need of a bike lock for your new bike you just purchased. Where would you go to find a lock for your new bike?

  • Target Success Criteria: Categories / Sports and Outdoors / Bikes / Bike Accessories
  • Walmart Success Criteria: Sports, Fitness & Outdoors / Bikes / Bike Accessories

Results

  • Target
    • Direct Success: 69.2%
    • Indirect Success: 7.7%
    • Direct Failure: 15.4%
    • Indirect Failure: 7.7%
  • Walmart
    • Direct Success: 57.1%
    • Indirect Success: 28.6%
    • Direct Failure: 14.3%

Finding 2

Users shop for bike locks in all Bike subcategories, such as Bike Tools & Maintenance, Adult Bikes and Bike Parts. Some users look for bike locks in the Industrial and Tires section.

In the Target study, the failed paths were:

  • Categories / Sports and Outdoors / Bikes / Adult Bikes 
  • Categories / Sports and Outdoors / Bikes / Bike Tools & Maintenance 
  • Categories / Sports and Outdoors / Bikes / Bike Parts (then this user went back to Bikes / Adult Bikes and then back to Bikes / Bike Parts) 

In the Walmart study, the failed path was:

  • Auto, Tires & Industrial / Tools & Equipment 

This is where participants clicked first:

  • Sports, Fitness and Outdoors (71%) Correct first click
  • Auto, Tires & Industrial (14%)
  • Patio and Garden (14%)

Recommendation 2

Target Recommendation: Include bike lock products under all Bike subcategories such as Adult Bikes, Bike Tools & Maintenance and Bike Parts, in addition to Bike Accessories.

Walmart Recommendation: It would be great to test if additional users would expect to find a bike lock in the Auto, Tires and Industrial section.

03. Task and Success Criteria

Your company has decided working from home is over. Starting next week you will go back to the office so you want to purchase a new lunch bag so that you can take your lunch to work. Where would you go to browse for lunch bags?

  • Target Success Criteria: Categories / Luggage / Lunch Bags
  • Walmart Success Criteria: Home, Furniture & Appliances / Shop All Home / Kitchen & Dining / Kitchen Storage

Results

  • Target
    • Direct Success: 38.5%
    • Indirect Success: 7.7%
    • Direct Failure: 15.4%
    • Indirect Failure: 38.5%
  • Walmart
    • Indirect Success: 14.3%
    • Indirect Failure: 85.7%

Finding 3

Users shop for lunch bags mostly in the Kitchen category. They also expect to find lunch bags in the Office, Household and Storage & Organization sections.

In the Target study:

  • 46% looked in the Kitchen and Dining category.
  • 23% looked in the Office and Supplies category.

Failed paths ended in:

  • Tableware (28%) 
  • Kitchen Utensiles & Gadgets (28%)
  • Kitchen Storage (28%) 
  • Cookware (14%)
  • Heating, Cooling & Air Quality (14%)

In the Walmart study:

  • 64% visited Food, Household, and Pets first and 82% during.
  • 55% visited Home, Furniture and Appliances (correct) during.
  • 18% visited Home for the Holidays first and 27% during.
  • 18% visited Electronics and Office first and 36% during.

Failed paths ended in:

  • Luggage and Travel (27%)
  • Household Essentials (18%)
  • Cookware (9%)
  • Storage and Organization (9%)
  • Dining Room (9%)
  • Home (9%)
  • Shop all Food (9%)

Recommendation 3

Target Recommendaiton: Include lunch bags under Kitchen and Dining as well as under Office and Supplies categories in the Target navigation. When looking in the Kitchen and Dining category, participants expected to find the lunch bag in either Tableware, Kitchen Utensils and Gadgets, Kitchen Storage or Cookware, evenly. Perhaps adding the Lunch bag to these categories or making Lunch bag its own subcategory under Kitchen and Dining and Office and Supplies could help the user find this item more efficiently.

Walmart Recommendation: Include lunch bags under Food, Household and Pets, Home for the Holidays and Electronics and Office categories in the Walmart navigation. Consider also including Lunch bags in Luggage and Travel.

04. Task and Success Criteria

Your coffee table just broke and you are looking to replace it. Where would you look for coffee tables?

  • Target Success Criteria: Categories / Furniture / Living Room Furniture / Coffee Tables
  • Walmart Success Criteria:
    • Home / Furniture & Appliances / Shop all Home / Furniture / Living Room Furniture
    • Home, Furniture & Appliances / Popular in Home / Coffee Tables
    • Home, Furniture & Appliances / Shop by Room / Furniture / Living Room Furniture

Results

  • Target
    • Direct Success: 53.8%
    • Indirect Success: 15.4%
    • Direct Failure: 15.4%
    • Indirect Failure: 15.4%
  • Walmart
    • Direct Success: 57.1%
    • Indirect Success: 14.3%
    • Direct Failure: 28.6%

Finding 4

Users shop for coffee tables in the deails section and in the Kitchen and Dining Furniture category too.

In the Target study, failed paths were:

  • Deals / Clearance
  • Deals / Top Deals
  • Categories / Househols Essentials and then back to Categories / Furniture and then back to Categories / Kitchen & Dining / Kitchen & Dining Furniture
  • Deals and then back to Menu / Categories / Home / Home Decor

This is where participants clicked first:

  • Categories (28%) Correct First Click 
  • Deals (75%)

In the Walmart study, failed paths were:

  • Home, Furniture & Appliances / Shop by Room / Kitchen & Dining
  • Home, Furniture & Appliances / Deals / Furniture under $100

Recommendation 4

When shopping for coffee tables, users want to search by deals. A participant expected to find coffee table in the Kitchen and Dining category. It would be interesting to test this further and to see if it's a trend. Most people nowadays live in an open space where their kitchen and dining is combined with living room. It would be interesting to see if this affects people's shopping behaviour.

Your mom's birthday is coming up and you want to buy her a perfume. Where would you find a perfume for your mom?

    • Categories / Beauty / Fragrances
    • Categories / Personal Care / Bath & Body / Body Mist
  • Walmart Success Criteria:
    • Beauty / Beauty / Fragrance

Results

  • Target

    • Indirect Success: 30.8%

    • Indirect Failure: 23.1%
  • Walmart
    • Direct Success: 71.4%
    • Direct Failure: 14.3%
    • Indirect Failure: 14.3%

Finding 5

Users shop for perfumes in the Womens section, such as the Women Accessories category. They also shop for perfumes in the Pharmacy and Personal Care sections.

In the Target study, 67% of failed paths were Categories / Women / Women Accessories. The rest of the failed paths ended in New in Womne's or Top Deals.

This is where participants clicked first:

  • Categories (77%) Correct first click
  • Deals (15%)
  • Registry (8%)

In the Walmart study, 50% of participants that had failed paths were looking for perfume in the Pharmacy, Health and Personal Care category. The other 50% in Gifts category or Beauty / Bestsellers.

This is where participants clicked first:

  • Beauty (55%) Correct first click 
  • Pharmacy, Health and Personal Care (27%)
  • Home for the Holidays (18%)

Recommendation 5

Include perfumes in Women Accessories in the Target navigation. Include perfumes in Pharmacy, Health and Personal Care category in the Walmart navigation.

06. Task and Success Criteria

It's your mom's birthday tomorrow and you'd like to buy her a birthday card. Where would you find this?

  • Target Success Criteria: Categories / Party Supplies / Cards & Stationery / Birthday Cards
  • Walmart Success Criteria:
    • Art, Craft, Sewing & Party Supplies / Party Supplies / Greeting & Thank You Cards
    • Art, Craft, Sewing & Party Supplies / Gift Wrap / Greeting Card

Results

  • Target
    • Indirect Success: 15.4%
    • Direct Failure: 46.2%
    • Indirect Failure: 38.5%
  • Walmart
    • Indirect Success: 14.3%
    • Direct Failure: 14.3%
    • Indirect Failure: 71.4%

Finding 6

Users shop for birthday cards in the Gift Card and Gift Ideas sections. They also shop for birthday cards in the party supplies section.

In the Target study, 100% of failed paths ended in Gift Card or Gift Ideas:

  • Categories / Gift Cards
  • Categories / Gift Ideas

In the Walmart study:

  • 54% of participants ended in Birthday Shop from Art, Craft, Sewing and Party Supplies
  • 18% of participants ended in Gift Finder from Gifts
  • 9% ended in Personalized Gifts from Gifts
  • 9% ended in Gift Cards from Gifts

This is where participants clicked first:

  • Gifts (64%) 
  • Art, Craft, Sewing and Party Supplies (27%) Correct first click

Recommendation 6

Target Recommendations: Gifts and greeting cards go hand in hand. Including birthday cards in the Gift Card and Gift Ideas categories in the Target navigation will help users find everything they need for a gift in one place.

Walmart Recommendations: Include birthday cards in the Gifts category in the Walmart navigation. Include birthday cards in Birthday Shop subcategory under Art, Craft, Sewing and Party Supplies.
